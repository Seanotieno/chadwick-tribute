Chadwick Boseman

The King Who Inspired Generation

Lifespan

November 29, 1976 – August 28, 2020

(Age 43)

An Iconic Career of Strength and Grace

Chadwick Boseman was more than just an actor; he was a vessel for history and a beacon of hope. He brought immense dignity to his portrayals of real-life American icons like Jackie Robinson in 42, James Brown in Get on Up, and Thurgood Marshall in Marshall.

However, he is most globally revered for bringing King T'Challa—the Black Panther—to life. He gave the world its first Black superhero leading a blockbuster film, creating a cultural phenomenon that celebrated African excellence and inspired millions of children to see themselves as heroes.

What makes his career truly iconic, and incredibly humbling, is knowing that he filmed many of his most demanding action roles, including Black Panther, Avengers: Infinity War, and Endgame, while secretly battling stage IV colon cancer. His perseverance, work ethic, and commitment to his art in the face of unimaginable adversity define true heroism.
